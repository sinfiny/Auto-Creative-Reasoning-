# Narrative Hook Hold Payoff

Use this skill to make scenes and chapters measurable: they should hook the reader, hold attention through escalation, and pay off with a turn, answer, wound, reveal, choice, or cliffhanger.

## Source Protocol

- Read `references/source-notes.md` for Steven Pressfield transcript-derived structure notes.
- Use the source as a story architecture lens, not as a formula or voice to imitate.

## Operating Loop

1. Name the unit: scene, chapter, act, arc, or whole story.
2. Define the Hook: entry conflict, question, image, promise, desire, or disruption.
3. Define the Hold: escalation, complication, villain pressure, midpoint turn, mystery deepening, or rising stakes.
4. Define the Payoff: resolution, cliffhanger, reversal, identity reveal, epiphany, cost, or next promise.
5. Draft or revise to serve those criteria.
6. Self-check; if the hold is flat or payoff unearned, repair before presenting.

## Structural Lenses

Use only the lenses that fit the user's story:

- Inciting incident: the moment the story starts moving and foreshadows the climax.
- Recurring mystery: the question or symbol that keeps returning.
- Midpoint turn: the moment stakes rise and the story takes on new meaning.
- All-is-lost moment: the enclosed pressure where escape looks impossible.
- Epiphany: the insight or shift that follows pressure.
- Hero/villain worldview: the conflict of values beneath the plot conflict.
- Ordinary/extraordinary world: the crossing into a changed arena.
- True identity: the moment a character reveals who they really are.
- Quiet solo moment: a low-noise beat that lets pressure resonate.
- Small meaningful stakes: intimate consequences that make large stakes felt.

## Rules

- Do not add lore when the problem is missing pressure.
- Do not add action when the problem is missing meaning.
- Do not add a twist when the problem is missing setup.
- Make the hero suffer only in ways that produce choice, learning, cost, or revelation.
- Preserve genre promises; refresh them with a new spin rather than rejecting them.
- End with a changed story state.
