# Mystery Payoff And Fairness Judge

Use this skill to judge whether a chapter, arc, or outline actually earns the feeling of depth, danger, and revelation that mystery-heavy strategic serials promise.

## Quick Start

- Read `references/scorecard.md` first.
- Use this after outlining, after drafting a chapter, or after a major reveal design pass.
- Pair with `$strategic-webserial-architecture` when the failure is structural rather than local.

## Operating Loop

1. Identify the active promises and open questions.
2. List the visible clues, hidden clues, and misleading interpretations currently on the page.
3. Check causal integrity: what happened because of prior incentives, information, and capabilities?
4. Check intelligence integrity: who acted below their established level, and why?
5. Check chapter value: what changed in knowledge, pressure, alignment, cost, or danger?
6. Score the work using the rubric in `references/scorecard.md`.
7. Name the highest-level fix first: prose, scene, chapter, arc, or system repair.

## Output Contract

```text
Scope judged:
Promises in play:
Best working element:
Major failures:
Score summary:
Highest-level fix needed:
Revision order:
Residual risks:
```

## Rules

- Findings come first. Do not soften major failures with vague praise.
- Do not reward obscurity. A fair mystery with tension beats a murky mystery with atmosphere.
- Do not call a twist "earned" unless the clues existed before the reveal.
- Do not excuse weak causality because the vibe is good.
- Separate local execution problems from architectural problems so the rewrite happens at the right level.
