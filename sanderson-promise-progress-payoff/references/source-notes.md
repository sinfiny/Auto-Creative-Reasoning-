# Source Notes

Primary lectures:

- Lecture 2: promise, progress, payoff
- Lecture 4: plot Q&A
- Lecture 5: plot FAQ

Condensed notes:

- Plot is both premise-level purpose and chapter-level motion.
- Promises include tone, plot, character, and world expectations.
- Progress is often where a book lives or dies because it dominates the reading experience.
- Payoff must satisfy the promise that mattered most, not just any problem on the page.
- Character arcs are also promises and often need to land immediately before or within the external climax.
- Bittersweet works when the story has prepared the reader early for mixed-cost outcomes.
- Scope can be changed by adding or removing arcs, characters, and locations.
- Internal and external conflict usually work best when braided together.
- Beta readers are often accurate about discomfort and inaccurate about the fix.

Operational implications:

- For a weak opening, identify missing promises.
- For a weak middle, identify invisible or repetitive progress.
- For a weak ending, identify the unpaid promise.
