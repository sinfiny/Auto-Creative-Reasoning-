# YC Thesis Memo

## Working Title

Autonomous Creative Reasoning for Serialized Fiction

## Core Thesis

Most AI writing products fail because they treat generation as the product.

Our view is the opposite: in creative writing, especially webserial fiction, the evaluator is the product. The generator is replaceable. The hard problem is building an autonomous loop that can tell whether a chapter became more addictive, more strategic, more emotionally compelling, and more likely to sustain long-term reader obsession.

This repository is the early proof of that thesis.

## Why This Matters

Serialized fiction is not judged the same way as essays or short stories. It wins on:

- chapter-to-chapter compulsion
- mystery carry-forward
- protagonist strategic appeal
- consequence density
- progression satisfaction
- reliable payoffs over long arcs

Today, most AI systems can generate plausible prose, but they cannot reliably improve those deeper serial qualities through iteration. They smooth language while weakening obsession.

If creative quality can be decomposed into explicit benchmark classes and rewrite decisions, then fiction can be iterated on more like code or research.

## Product Insight

The company is not "an AI novelist."

The company is a creative optimization engine:

- generation layer
- evaluation layer
- control layer

That system can first target webserial fiction, then expand into scripts, games, fanfiction tooling, adaptation planning, and editor copilots.

## Why This Founder

The founder story is not pedigree-driven.

It is obsession-driven.

No social degree, no prior institutional credibility, no inherited access. The edge comes from seeing a hard problem clearly and refusing to treat it as impossible just because the category sounds subjective. The founder insight is that creative judgment can be operationalized enough to drive autonomous improvement.

That kind of conviction is only valuable if it becomes visible in artifacts. This repo is meant to become those artifacts:

- benchmark suites
- skill systems
- loop contracts
- before/after proof cases
- public demonstrations of judged improvement

## Why Now

- LLM generation quality is high enough that evaluation, not raw text generation, is the bottleneck.
- Agent frameworks make iterative loops practical.
- Web-native fiction markets reward measurable reader retention and long-form compulsion.
- Most competitors still optimize for one-shot novelty or style mimicry.

## Initial Wedge

Autonomous improvement for webserial fiction:

- progression fantasy
- strategic fantasy
- occult mystery
- highly serialized chapter-based fiction

This wedge is attractive because:

- quality is legible in reader behavior
- chapter units are naturally loopable
- retention and payoff matter more than literary prestige signals
- the market is underserved by serious creation tooling

## Proof Plan

The near-term proof should be:

1. A public benchmark for serial-fiction quality.
2. An autonomous loop that improves benchmark scores over successive passes.
3. Before/after cases where the improvement is obvious.
4. Early distribution experiments with real readers or platform posts.
5. A narrative that the system is building evaluative intelligence, not just producing text.

## What Success Looks Like

Success is not "the AI wrote a perfect novel."

Success is:

- the system can repeatedly improve a chapter under stable judging criteria
- the system can explain why a draft failed
- the system chooses the right rewrite level
- the improvement transfers across benchmark classes
- real readers prefer the revised version often enough to matter

## Risks

- false confidence from bad rubrics
- optimization toward smoothness instead of obsession
- confusing atmospheric obscurity with real mystery
- premature claims before benchmark proof exists

## Strategic Discipline

Avoid claiming full autonomy too early.

The strongest framing is:

- we are building the benchmarked control system for creative iteration
- fiction is the first domain
- the repo is an early research artifact, not the final product
