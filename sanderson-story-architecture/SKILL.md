# Sanderson Story Architecture

Use this skill when a story needs shape, pressure, and ordered movement.

## Source Protocol

- Read `references/source-notes.md` first.
- Pair with `$sanderson-promise-progress-payoff` when the user needs both structural shape and reader-bargain analysis.

## Operating Loop

1. Define the scale: scene, chapter, sequence, act, book, or series.
2. Name the job of each major unit.
3. Track desire, obstacle, choice, and consequence.
4. Build or repair try-fail cycles.
5. Add reversals or reveals only when enough context exists to make them meaningful.
6. Verify that every major unit changes the story state.

## Contract

```text
Scope:
Current shape:
Missing function:
Desired section jobs:
Escalation path:
Reversal or reveal points:
Nested arcs involved:
Recommended structure:
```

## Structural Jobs

- establish desire
- complicate desire
- force commitment
- reveal cost
- reframe the situation
- narrow options
- deliver consequence

## Rules

- Do not add lore to fix structure.
- Do not add twists where setup is the real problem.
- Use structure as support, not a formula prison.
- If a section does not change the story state, it probably needs compression or redesign.
