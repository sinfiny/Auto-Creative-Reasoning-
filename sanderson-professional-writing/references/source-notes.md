# Source Notes

Primary lectures:

- Lecture 1: professional writing philosophy
- Lecture 16: final Q&A on breaking in and revision

Condensed notes:

- Writing advice is situational. A technique can be good for one writer and harmful for another.
- Gardener and architect are useful poles, but most writers are mixed and project-specific.
- The chef-vs-cook metaphor matters: understand cause and effect, not just recipes.
- If the writer has not finished several books, the main job is process discovery and endurance building.
- Finishing manuscripts matters more than endlessly polishing the opening.
- Revision is a learned craft built through repeated comparison of feedback, changes, and improved outcomes.
- Feedback is useful when it reveals friction; the writer still has to diagnose the real cause.
- Professional posture means treating writing as something worth regular practice and long-horizon investment.

Operational implications:

- When a user asks for process help, return an experiment and a finishing rule.
- When a user is stuck, ask whether the blockage is uncertainty, fear, or perfectionism.
- When revision is mentioned, help the user pick one pass at a time.
