# Clarity And Visuality Editor

Use this skill to make nonfiction easier to see and understand.

## Source Protocol

- Read `references/source-notes.md` when the user wants the source rationale or the exact editing lens behind this skill.
- Use the source material as a clarity workflow, not as a style to mimic.
- Do not imitate David Perell, Steven Pinker, or any living writer's voice.

## Use When

- The writing feels abstract or bloodless.
- The reader cannot picture what the text means.
- The draft sounds smart but lands weakly.

## Do Not Use When

- The user wants ornate rhetoric or poetic density.
- The draft is already intentionally spare and concrete.
- The task is mainly structural rather than sentence-level.

## Attention Targets

- Concrete nouns and verbs
- Visual language
- Context before example
- Reader knowledge gaps
- Unnecessary compression or jargon
- Bland AI phrasing

## Operating Rules

- Replace vague abstractions with observable examples where possible.
- Give examples only after the reader has enough frame to interpret them.
- Prefer familiar language unless technical precision is required.
- Cut filler, throat-clearing, and inflated phrasing.
- Preserve the idea's nuance while improving legibility.
- Explain why a sentence is unclear, not only how to rewrite it.

## Failure Modes

- Oversimplifying the idea
- Replacing precision with hand-wavy friendliness
- Adding examples that do not match the claim
- Flattening author voice into generic polish
