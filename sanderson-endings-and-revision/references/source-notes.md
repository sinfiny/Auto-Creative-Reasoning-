# Source Notes

Primary lectures:

- Lecture 4 and 5: payoff and bittersweet questions
- Lecture 16: final Q&A on endings, revision, and breaking in

Condensed notes:

- A satisfying ending pays off the promise that mattered most.
- Character payoff often needs to land directly adjacent to the external payoff.
- Bittersweet can work if the tone contract is established early.
- Revision is learned through repeated experimentation and comparison, not by theory alone.
- Beta readers are good at noticing friction and poor at prescribing exact fixes.
- Alpha readers can be more directive when they are skilled and trusted.
- A writer should stop revising blindly once new drafts become sideways moves instead of real improvements.
- Studying draft-to-final changes can help, but direct revision experience matters more.

Operational implications:

- When the ending fails, trace the unpaid promise.
- When revision is vague, choose one pass and define its scope.
- When feedback is noisy, look for recurring pain points instead of obeying one loud reader.
