# Source Notes

Primary lectures:

- Lecture 9: Sanderson's laws of magic
- Lecture 10: tools to make worlds better
- Lecture 11: how to worldbuild on Earth
- Lecture 12: worldbuilding Q&A

Condensed notes:

- A satisfying magical resolution depends on how well the reader understands the system.
- Hard and soft magic are tools for different emotional outcomes.
- Internal logic matters more than pseudo-scientific surface explanation.
- Magical systems should generate costs, choices, and consequences.
- Worldbuilding should support plot and character rather than exist as self-contained trivia.
- Concrete details often do more work than grand abstract cosmology.
- The easiest way to make worldbuilding matter is to make it matter to a character.
- Discovery writers should build world detail just in time, not all at once.
- Reader orientation should usually happen through active scenes, not static exposition.

Operational implications:

- For a vague setting, ask what changes because this setting exists.
- For a magic climax, ask what was seeded early enough for fair payoff.
- For a lore-heavy draft, cut anything that does not alter behavior or stakes.
