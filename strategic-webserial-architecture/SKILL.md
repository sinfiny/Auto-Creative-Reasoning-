# Strategic Webserial Architecture

Use this skill when the story needs long-range shape, layered curiosity, and reveals that feel earned instead of random.

## Quick Start

- Use this before drafting when the story depends on nested mysteries, faction schemes, hidden pasts, or late payoffs.
- Pair with `$faction-power-consequence-engine` when advancement systems or organizations are driving the plot.
- Pair with `$mystery-payoff-and-fairness-judge` when you need to stress-test whether the outline is actually fair and satisfying.

## Operating Loop

1. Define the promise stack: survival, advancement, hidden truth, faction conflict, and world-depth promises.
2. Separate visible goal from hidden arc. The protagonist may chase one thing while the reader is slowly being led toward a larger truth.
3. Build a question ladder at three levels: chapter question, arc question, series question.
4. For each major reveal, place the earlier seeds that make it possible to believe in hindsight.
5. Track clue function. Every clue should confirm, distort, redirect, or unlock.
6. Make each arc ending close at least one active question while opening a larger or more dangerous one.
7. Check that the story state changes irreversibly after major discoveries, victories, and losses.

## Mystery Ladder Heuristics

- The chapter should answer something small, complicate something medium, and sharpen something large.
- A reveal is stronger when it changes meaning, not just information.
- Reader confusion is not the same as mystery. If the reader cannot track causal logic, clarity comes first.
- Hidden history should keep changing present strategy. If old lore has no current cost, it is background, not plot.

## Rules

- Do not add twists to compensate for weak setup.
- Do not add lore dumps where a clue chain would do better.
- Do not solve every mystery at the same narrative altitude.
- Preserve uncertainty, but always know what the reader is meant to wonder about next.
- Do not imitate exact scenes, labels, cosmology, or plot turns from Reverend Insanity or Lord of the Mysteries. Use them as structural inspiration only.
