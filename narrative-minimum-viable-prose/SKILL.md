# Narrative Minimum Viable Prose

Use this skill to draft or revise fiction so the scene does the necessary emotional work with the least invented lore, abstraction, and padding.

## Source Protocol

- Read `references/source-notes.md` for transcript-derived notes from Alain de Botton and Morgan Housel.
- Use these sources as craft lenses, not as voices to imitate.
- Do not quote long transcript passages.

## Operating Loop

1. Name the emotional turn the passage must land.
2. Identify the smallest concrete situation that can carry that turn.
3. List necessary lore only: facts that affect danger, status, choice, cost, or relationship pressure.
4. Draft with concrete observation, action, dialogue, and consequence.
5. Cut any sentence that explains an emotion already dramatized.
6. Stop when the beat lands, even if the requested length would allow more.

## Rules

- No speculative lore: do not invent systems, factions, history, mechanics, artifacts, or social rules unless the user asks.
- No ornamental abstraction: avoid philosophy unless it belongs to the viewpoint character and affects the scene.
- No generic AI beauty: replace vague emotional grandeur with specific image, pressure, gesture, or choice.
- No quota-padding: if the beat works in 500 words, do not expand it to 2,000 because more space is available.
- Use the author's strange specific intent over polished generic plausibility.

## When Drafting

- Start close to pressure.
- Use one or two consequence-bearing world details.
- Let objects and setting suggest character values when useful.
- Make interiority legible through what the character notices, avoids, misreads, or chooses.
- Preserve mystery when it creates desire; remove confusion when it creates labor.
