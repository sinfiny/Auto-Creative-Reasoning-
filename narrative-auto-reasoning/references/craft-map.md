# Craft Map

This reference condenses the source interviews into practical controls for AI-assisted fiction. Use it as an operating model, not as a voice or style target.

## Four Control Loops

### Think Before Drafting

Purpose: prevent silent assumptions from becoming canon.

- Name the character's emotional goal before action.
- Name the outward goal before blocking or escalating it.
- List lore constraints before inventing new material.
- If the prompt asks for a major twist, surface multiple interpretations before choosing one.
- Halt when the requested beat contradicts known motivation, timeline, or lore.

### Simplicity First

Purpose: keep prose from becoming inflated, abstract, or lore-heavy.

- Draft the minimum scene that lands the emotional turn.
- Introduce only world details that change behavior, danger, status, cost, or choice.
- Replace generic beauty or philosophy with specific observation.
- Cut anything that explains what the scene already dramatizes.
- Prefer one charged object, gesture, or line over a paragraph of explanation.

### Surgical Changes

Purpose: preserve existing story structure when editing.

- Define the edit radius before changing prose.
- Keep untouched beats, formatting, and pacing intact.
- When removing an element, remove direct dangling references but do not rewrite unrelated story spine.
- Preserve the genre bargain unless the user asks to change it.
- Report exactly what changed.

### Goal-Driven Execution

Purpose: make every generation loop measurable.

- Hook: the entry conflict, question, image, desire, or disruption.
- Hold: the escalation that keeps the reader leaning forward.
- Payoff: the turn, answer, wound, reveal, reversal, choice, or cliffhanger.
- Self-check against these criteria after drafting.
- Rewrite before presenting if the payoff is flat or context-breaking.

## Narrative State Model

Treat a story as a live graph of:

- Characters
- Goals
- Wounds
- Secrets
- Relationships
- Lore constraints
- Promises
- Open loops
- Payoffs
- Forbidden contradictions

Before adding prose, identify which nodes are active and which edges must not change. A scene may update the graph, but it should not mutate unrelated canon by accident.

## Scene Contract

Every scene must answer:

- Who wants what now?
- What pressure blocks them?
- What changes by the end?
- What does the reader now expect?
- What must not be contradicted?

If one answer is missing, plan before drafting. If the scene has no change, compress it, merge it, or convert it into summary.

## Drafting Heuristics

- Motivation before motion.
- Consequence before lore.
- Specific image before abstraction.
- Choice before explanation.
- Compression before expansion.
- Payoff in the same emotional currency as the hook.
- Concrete pressure before interior summary.
- Reader orientation before mystery.
- Productive mystery over accidental confusion.

## Failure Modes

- Silent assumption: the draft invents motivation, history, or lore as if it were established.
- Lore sprawl: the scene stops to explain systems that do not affect the immediate beat.
- Purple drift: the prose decorates the emotion instead of dramatizing it.
- Emotional contradiction: the character performs a feeling the story has not earned.
- Edit-radius creep: a small requested change turns into a chapter rewrite.
- Genre shame: the draft tries to "improve" genre conventions by sanding off the thing readers came for.
- Aimless middle: events happen, but Hook/Hold/Payoff cannot be named.
- Payoff without setup: the ending solves a problem with tools, emotions, or rules the reader never received.
- Twist that breaks the story contract: surprise comes from contradiction rather than recontextualization.

## Fast Checks

- What is the smallest version of this scene that still hurts, delights, reveals, or turns?
- Which one lore detail changes what a character can do?
- What assumption am I making that the user did not give me?
- What line, gesture, or choice proves the emotional state?
- What reader promise does this beat create, progress, or pay off?
- What should remain untouched?
