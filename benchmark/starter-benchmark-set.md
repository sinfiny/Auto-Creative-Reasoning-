# Starter Benchmark Set

This is a starter set for evaluating whether the system can improve webserial writing through iteration.

Use these as benchmark cards, not as final story premises to publish unchanged.

## Opening Chapters

### O1. Strategic Survivor

- Premise: A low-status protagonist discovers a system exploit that could save them, but using it will expose them to a ruthless institution.
- Must test:
  - first-chapter compulsion
  - protagonist strategic appeal
  - visible cost of opportunity

### O2. Forbidden Knowledge Entry

- Premise: The protagonist gains access to a piece of hidden knowledge that instantly reframes their world, but they understand only a fragment of it.
- Must test:
  - mystery carry-forward
  - clue clarity without overexplaining
  - atmosphere with function

### O3. Ambition Under Constraint

- Premise: The protagonist wants social ascent in a rigid hierarchy and makes a morally dubious first move to get it.
- Must test:
  - character agency
  - cost-bearing ambition
  - chapter-end propulsion

## Reveal Scenes

### R1. Recontextualizing Ally

- Premise: A trusted ally is revealed to have been truthful in words but deceptive in framing.
- Must test:
  - fairness of prior clues
  - meaning shift
  - consequence density

### R2. Power System Trap

- Premise: The protagonist learns that a major advancement method creates a hidden long-term dependency.
- Must test:
  - system depth
  - future strategic consequences
  - payoff inevitability

### R3. Ancient History With Present Cost

- Premise: A buried historical truth changes which faction currently benefits from the status quo.
- Must test:
  - lore-to-strategy conversion
  - faction dynamism
  - arc escalation

## Strategic Confrontations

### S1. Negotiation Under Surveillance

- Premise: Two smart actors bargain while both suspect a third party is listening.
- Must test:
  - intelligence integrity
  - leverage use
  - subtext and misdirection

### S2. Controlled Loss

- Premise: The protagonist chooses a deliberate short-term loss to gain a stronger future position.
- Must test:
  - strategic reasoning visibility
  - consequence credibility
  - reader trust

### S3. Rival Mirror Match

- Premise: The antagonist reads the protagonist correctly and forces a move that still leaves room for a countermove.
- Must test:
  - non-stupid opposition
  - pressure escalation
  - no cheap author rescue

## Arc Scaffolds

### A1. Rising Institution Threat

- Arc target: Show how one institution closes around the protagonist over 12-20 chapters.
- Must test:
  - serial escalation
  - recurring pressure without repetition
  - interim payoffs

### A2. Expedition Into Hidden Layer

- Arc target: Move from rumor to entry to partial truth without collapsing mystery too early.
- Must test:
  - question ladder discipline
  - reveal spacing
  - environment as pressure

### A3. Social Ascension Arc

- Arc target: The protagonist climbs status by exploiting faction fractures while gaining enemies.
- Must test:
  - strategic progression
  - social consequence
  - reversible vs irreversible wins
