# Scorecard

Use a 1-5 score for each category.

## Categories

- Promise clarity: Does the reader know what kinds of answers, dangers, and satisfactions the story is promising?
- Curiosity ladder: Do chapter, arc, and larger-series questions all remain active and distinct?
- Clue fairness: Were enough seeds present for the reveal to feel earned?
- Meaning shift: Does a reveal change interpretation, not just add trivia?
- Causal integrity: Do outcomes follow from incentives, information, and capability?
- Strategic intelligence: Do key actors behave at the level the story claims?
- Consequence density: Do wins, losses, and discoveries permanently alter future options?
- Faction pressure: Do organizations feel like active powers instead of scenery?
- Chapter value: Does the chapter add discovery, pressure, cost, or irreversible change?
- Atmosphere with function: Does the eerie, ancient, or mythic material affect decisions and risk?
- Payoff inevitability: Is the twist surprising now but obvious in hindsight?

## Thresholds

- `5`: Strong enough to keep. Only polish or sharpen.
- `4`: Works well but has visible slack.
- `3`: Serviceable, but the story will feel ordinary or leaky if not revised.
- `2`: Important weakness. The reader may feel convenience, drift, or fake complexity.
- `1`: Failing category. Structural rethink required.

## Red Flags

- A reveal depends on facts never seeded.
- The protagonist wins because everyone else overlooks the obvious.
- A faction disappears whenever the plot needs privacy.
- Advancement creates no new vulnerability.
- Atmosphere is present, but it does not change strategy.
- The chapter ends on suspense, but nothing meaningful changed.

## Revision Priority

Fix in this order:

1. Causal integrity
2. Strategic intelligence
3. Clue fairness
4. Consequence density
5. Curiosity ladder
6. Atmosphere and prose
