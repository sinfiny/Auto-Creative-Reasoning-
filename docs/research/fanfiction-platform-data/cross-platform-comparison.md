## Platform-Specific Signatures

### SpaceBattles

- **Forum-driven discussion culture** — engagement measured in pages of discussion, not just views
- **Worm as universal solvent** — 21% of all readers; nearly any other franchise can cross with it
- **SI/crossover as default** — roughly half the top 30 are self-inserts or crossovers
- **Military/command fantasy** — Imperial Governor (Star Wars), Mech pilot (Battletech), Gue'vesa (WH40K)
- **Original fiction growing** — 5.9% reader share; chat fic and xianxia emerging
- **Author personality matters** — Seras dominates across both SB and RR simultaneously

### Royal Road

- **Original fiction dominant** — fan fiction is the exception, not the rule
- **Progression/LitRPG is the baseline** — nearly every top fiction has numbers going up
- **Patreon economy** — advance chapters are standard; RR is the money platform
- **Cultivation synthesis** — Western progression fantasy + xianxia/xuanhuan blending
- **Genre tag taxonomy** — consistent, searchable, filterable (unlike SB)
- **Long serial commitment** — 300–1,000+ chapters is normal for ongoing top stories

### Webnovel

- **Chinese web novel fandoms** — Douluo Dalu, Three Kingdoms have no equivalent on SB/RR
- **System/cheat power fantasy** — universal; "I can upgrade everything" is the default
- **Harem romance** — openly tagged, commonly expected
- **Massive chapter counts** — 600–2,000+ chapters; webnovel pacing is fast
- **Translation layer** — many titles are translated Chinese originals alongside English ones
- **Rating system** — lower averages (2.85–4.61 range) compared to RR's higher baseline

### Questionable Questing

- **SB's NSFW mirror** — same fandoms, same SI/crossover engine, explicit content allowed
- **CYOA as format** — unique interactive thread type; builds feed into stories
- **Romance-first** — where SB has subplot, QQ has primary genre
- **Login wall** — cannot be scraped; community knowledge is anecdotal
- **Smaller but intense** — fewer total stories, but very active core user base

## Key Takeaways

1. **SI/Isekai is the universal format.** Every platform, every fandom, every genre — the self-insert or portal fantasy framing dominates.

2. **Worm is the #1 crossover hub** on English-language forums, but **Naruto** and **Douluo Dalu** dominate Chinese-language platforms (Webnovel).

3. **Progression Fantasy / LitRPG** is the defining RR genre and increasingly bleeds into SB and WN. Numbers going up is the reader expectation.

4. **Cultivation is converging.** Western progression + xianxia mechanics appear on all four platforms; SB's "Beware of Chicken" and "Heroine's Master" mirror RR's "Sky Pride" and "Cultivating Common Sense."

5. **Original fiction is rising on SB** (5.9% share, up from near zero), but RR remains the primary home for original serial fiction with monetization.

6. **Cross-platform authors** like Seras (Ghost in the City, Mech Otaku's Touch) dominate by maintaining presence on both SB and RR simultaneously.

7. **Military/command SI** (Star Wars governor, BattleTech commander, WH40K officer) is uniquely strong on SpaceBattles.

8. **Chat/social media format** (Hero's Group Chat, Live at the Forum) is a SB trend with no equivalent on other platforms.

9. **Crossover absurdism** (Naruto/The Muppets, Worm/Musical, Pokémon/Worm) is an SB signature that doesn't translate to RR's original-fiction focus.

10. **The Patreon-to-RR pipeline** means RR authors can sustain full-time writing; SB and WN authors largely cannot (SB) or rely on subscription models (WN).
