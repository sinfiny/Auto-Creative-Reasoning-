# Starter Demo Case

## Case Name

Repairing a weak opening chapter for strategic webserial fiction

## Goal

Improve a baseline opening so it feels more bingeable and strategically compelling without bloating the prose.

## Benchmark Setup

- benchmark card: `O1. Strategic Survivor`
- rubric focus:
  - opening compulsion
  - protagonist agency
  - visible tradeoffs
  - chapter-end propulsion
- stop condition:
  - no focus category below `4`

## Before

Baseline pattern:

- protagonist is interesting in theory but mostly reactive
- a system opportunity exists but its cost is vague
- prose is readable but generic
- the chapter ends on information, not pressure

Indicative score:

- opening compulsion: `2`
- protagonist agency: `3`
- visible tradeoffs: `2`
- chapter-end propulsion: `2`

Main failure:

- the chapter promises potential, but it does not force a hard decision quickly enough

## Intervention

Rewrite level:

- chapter rewrite

What changed:

- moved the exploit opportunity earlier
- made the institution's threat immediate and legible
- forced the protagonist to choose whether to take a tainted advantage
- ended with a decision that creates future enemies

## After

Target result:

- the protagonist acts instead of merely noticing
- the opportunity has a visible cost
- the system feels useful and dangerous
- the ending hook comes from consequence, not vagueness

Indicative score:

- opening compulsion: `4`
- protagonist agency: `4`
- visible tradeoffs: `4`
- chapter-end propulsion: `4`

## Why This Matters

This case demonstrates the central claim of the harness:

- the loop does not just polish sentences
- it identifies that the real problem is chapter-level structure
- it applies the right rewrite level
- it produces a measurable improvement on a serial-fiction benchmark
