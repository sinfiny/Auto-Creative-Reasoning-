# Copywriting Mechanics

Use this skill for persuasive commercial writing.

## Source Protocol

- Read `references/source-notes.md` when the user asks where the tests or heuristics came from.
- Treat the source as a set of claim-strength checks, not a brand voice to cosplay.
- Do not invent evidence or unsupported product claims.

## Use When

- The user needs clearer, sharper, more persuasive marketing copy.
- A headline, slogan, CTA, or offer feels generic.
- A landing page explains but does not create desire.

## Do Not Use When

- The task is literary fiction, memoir, or poetry.
- The user wants a neutral summary instead of persuasion.
- The product claim cannot be supported.

## Attention Targets

- Specificity
- Visualizability
- Falsifiability
- Distinctiveness
- Reader desire
- Offer clarity
- Friction in headlines and CTAs

## Operating Rules

- Prefer concrete claims over brand fog.
- Ask whether the reader can picture the claim.
- Ask whether the claim could be proven false.
- Ask whether a competitor could say the same thing.
- Favor sharp benefits and reader outcomes over company self-description.
- Generate multiple angles before choosing one.
- If the source material is weak, say the positioning is weak instead of inventing substance.

## Failure Modes

- Empty slogans
- Hype without evidence
- Cleverness that hides the offer
- Copy that sounds like any other company
