# The Bell Under Glass

The market bell rang very softly beneath the glass roof, and Mara counted the echo because the echo always told her who was lying.

Jon kept his smile near the front of his face. He had learned that a little delay could make kindness look like strategy.

At the spice stall, the old magistrate placed three copper moons on the counter and asked for salt. Everyone knew salt had been illegal since the river turned silver.

Mara wanted to run, but the bell rang again behind her, closer this time, as if the glass roof had lowered by an inch.

The boy with the ink-stained cuffs moved from the back of the crowd to the front. He did not look at the magistrate. He looked at Mara's hands.
