# Source Notes

This skill is mainly grounded in Will Storr's long-form storytelling interview material as summarized in `David-Perell-writing-skills-proof.md`.

Use it for:

- character-led outlines
- stories built from contradiction, desire, and change
- scene and act pressure that cashes out in transformation

Core conversion:

- story starts with a person under pressure, not with plot furniture
- external conflict should expose or intensify an internal lack
- the ending should prove who the character became, refused to become, or failed to become

Keep the boundary narrow. This is not a universal fiction skill; it is a character-motivation and transformation tool.
